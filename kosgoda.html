KOSGODA TURTLE HATCHERIE _________________________________________________




ABOUT

Six of the seven species of sea turtles are now in risk of extinction. The majority of it results from the exploitation of natural resources by humans. Thankfully, this hatchery has accepted responsibility for their protection. Beginning with watching over the nesting mother turtles, they next transport their eggs to the safe environment of the hatchery and, on the day of birth, release the young turtles into the sea. If there are problems, they tend to them and only release them when they are well enough to enter the huge ocean. All five of the country's species can be found making their nests at Kosgoda, one of the relatively few hatcheries. They are the Hawksbill, Leatherback, Green, and Olive Ridley turtles.

HIGHLIGHTS

.You may witness five different species of turtles at the hatchery that breed near Kosgoda Beach.

.Learn about the care given to injured or ill adult turtles so they can recover.

.Learn how to gather the eggs or young turtles that are taken for safety from the shore

.knowledge of handling and feeding turtles

.If possible, during the season, release baby turtles that are prepared to return to the ocean

USEFUL INFORMATIONS

The Turtle Conservatory is open every day until 5:00 p.m., and admission is LKR 500.

After arriving, guests can spend 30 to 1 hour exploring the area and learning more about turtles, including their life cycles and the hatching process.

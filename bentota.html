BENTOTA TURTLE HATCHERY____________________________

ABOUT

To prevent the extinction of the turtles, the Sri Lankans established the Turtle Hatchery. In the past, people—typically fishermen—would gather turtle eggs and sell them to anyone willing to pay a greater price. These eggs were fed and cared for after the turtle hatchery opened until they were 2 to 4 years old. There are numerous turtle species, including the Green Turtle, Leatherback, and Olive Ridley. Despite being extinct, fishermen continue to catch turtles for their shells and fish, which is a very lucrative market.

HIGHLIGHTS

.Protecting turtle eggs and hatchlings from predators and other dangers is the goal of the turtle hatchery, a conservation initiative

.Visitors can interact with the turtles at the hatchery by holding and feeding them

.A well-liked tourist destination, the turtle hatchery is an excellent location for families with young children to learn about sea turtle conservation efforts and their life cycle.

USEFUL INFORMATIONS

The Turtle Conservatory is open every day until 5:00 p.m., and admission is LKR 500. After arriving, guests can spend 30 to 1 hour exploring the area and learning more about turtles, including their life cycles and the process of hatching.
